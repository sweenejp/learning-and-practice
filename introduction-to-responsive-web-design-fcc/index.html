The Razor's Edge

I’ll leave it there for now. That actually wasn’t as hard as I thought. I had the idea because I was worried that I forget everything about the books I read. It’d be a shame to forget everything you read - then why read it at all? Maybe because it’s intrinsically pleasing to read. Even if I forget everything about a book, was it still worth it to read it? Isn’t it worth it if I enjoyed reading it at the time?

I actually had a similar thought while at the cabin this year. While looking through old pictures of us, there were a few that I totally didn’t recognize. It got me thinking about memory. Do we enjoy things just because we remember them? Other people have brought up similar questions. It reminds me of the anesthesia vs induced amnesia thought experiment. Obviously I would choose anesthesia, but is it that much different if you just don’t remember it?>

One thing I know for sure is that it is fun to recall good memories. We did a bunch of that at Greg’s wedding and at the cabin this summer. So yet another reason to keep this journal. It will hopefully help me remember things.
